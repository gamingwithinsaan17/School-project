Ice and Water Under Threat in India

The Cryosphere in Crisis: Ladakh's Melting Glaciers

The Himalayan glaciers, often called Asia's "water towers," are retreating at an alarming rate. Ladakh, a cold desert region, is on the front lines, facing dire consequences for its water security and ecological stability.

10x

Faster Retreat

The retreat rate of the Parkachik glacier has accelerated tenfold from an average of 2m/year (1971-99) to 20m/year (2015-21).

Accelerating Glacier Retreat

The rate of ice loss from the Parkachik Glacier shows a dramatic increase in recent decades, highlighting the urgent impact of climate change.

The Looming Threat of GLOFs

Rapid melting creates unstable glacial lakes. A breach can cause a Glacial Lake Outburst Flood (GLOF), a catastrophic event threatening downstream communities. The Parkachik glacier alone could form 3 new lakes, significantly increasing this risk.

Water Security at Risk

Ladakh's agriculture and drinking water are heavily dependent on glacial melt. As these frozen reservoirs shrink, the region faces a future of severe water scarcity.

The Lifeline is Choking: Karnataka's Polluted Ghats

The Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage site and a global biodiversity hotspot, are facing a severe pollution crisis. The rivers that originate here, lifelines for millions, are being contaminated by a variety of human activities.

Major Sources of River Pollution

A combination of factors contributes to the degradation of the pristine rivers of the Western Ghats.
